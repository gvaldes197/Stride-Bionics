About the Strider

The Strider is a lightweight, wearable robotic device that provides powered assistance to the lower limbs for stroke survivors. Unlike rehab clinic systems, Strider is designed for unsupervised use with built-in fall detection, self-balancing control, and a user-friendly setup app.

No bulky frame. No upper limb motors. Just walking — made possible.

This product is under development and has not been cleared by the FDA.

Pricing Models We Are Exploring

1. Robotics-as-a-Service (RaaS)

A monthly subscription including the exoskeleton, support, and remote updates. Perfect for short-term rehab settings or trials.

2. Direct Purchase

Outright purchase model with extended service contracts. Ideal for hospitals or rehab centers investing in long-term use.

3. Lease-to-Own (Community Access)

A lease model for patients discharged from rehab, with monthly payments that apply toward ownership. Designed for home-based recovery.

Pricing models are not active. The product is not cleared by the FDA and cannot be purchased, leased, or distributed until authorized.
